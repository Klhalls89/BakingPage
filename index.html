PepparKakor

Swedish Spice Cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar (firmly packed)
  • 1/2 cup light molasses
  • 1 egg
  • 3 cups all purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp grated orange peel
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda

Directions

Making Dough

Yeild 6 dozen. Cream butter; gradually add sugars; beat until light & fluffy. Beat in molasses and egg. Combine flour, salt, spices, orange peel & baking soda. Gradually add to creamed mixture.Gather dough into a ball; wrap in plastic wrap. Chill 2-3 hours.

Baking Cookies

Preheat oven to 375 degrees. (Use 1/4 of dough at a time and keep the rest refrigerated until needed.) Sprinkle pastry cloth, dough and rolling pin with granulated sugar. Roll dough to 1/8” thick.(We use granulated sugar instead of flour for rolling out dough because it make the cookies crisp and sparkly) Cut with goat and pig-shaped cookie cutters. Place 1” apart on unbuttered cookie sheet. Bake 10 -12 minutes. Remove from cookie sheet and cool completely on wire racks. Decorate with colorful icing and sprinkles for eyes.

Tid Bits

“Peppar”comes from the Swedish word for many spices or pepper. Kakor means cookies. They are shaped most often like hearts or animals. The pig is a symbol of good luck and the goats pulled Thor’s chariot. My grandma liked to do dala horse cookies. I am adding cat shapes because Freya’s chariot was pulled by cats.
